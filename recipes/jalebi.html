How to make Jalebi

Home

Description

A delightful Indian dessert, jalebi is made by deep-frying a fermented batter into spiral shapes and then soaking them in saffron-infused sugar syrup. The result is a crispy, juicy, and syrupy treat with a perfect balance of sweetness and tang. Served hot, jalebi is enjoyed at festivals, special occasions, or simply as an indulgent snack with milk or rabri.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour (maida)
  • Sugar
  • Saffron
  • Yogurt
  • Oil

Steps

  1. Make the dough : Mix flour, cornflour, yogurt, baking soda, and water into a smooth, flowing consistency. Let it ferment for 4-5 hours.
  2. Make the filling: Heat sugar and water in a pan, add saffron, cardamom, and rose water, and simmer until slightly sticky. Keep warm.
  3. Fry: Pour the batter into a piping bag or squeeze bottle. Heat oil, pipe spirals into the hot oil, and fry until golden and crispy.
  4. Serve: Dip the fried jalebis in warm sugar syrup for 1-2 minutes, then serve hot.
